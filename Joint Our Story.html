The Challenge

How might we provide education and training to help the women of Kalma camp earn an income to support themselves and their children in a dignified and sustainable way?

The Problem

Malnutrition

20 million children will suffer from acute malnutrition this year. In Sudan only, 300,000 children are experiencing life-threatening severe wasting. This year alone, ARC will treat 5,100 suffering from severe and moderate acute malnutrition in Kalma Camp.

Ready-to-Use Therapeutic Foods (RUTFs) could get these children through malnutrition. UNICEF anticipates purchasing 34,000 metric tons (MT) of peanut-based RUTFs during 2014, but that is not nearly enough.

The current supply of RUTF is not able to keep up with the huge and urgent demand.

Lack of Opportunity

Currently, the most profitable option for women in Kalma camp is manual brick-making. It involves mixing animal waste, dirt, and water by hand in 100+ degree weather. At best, a woman can make $2.00 per day with this job.

Other jobs involve farming outside the camp, domestic work in the nearby city of Nyala, or as volunteer teaching assistants for small rations of food. However, all of these have involved high safety and security risks, including sexual assault.

None of these are nearly enough for the women to support themselves or their families.

The Opportunity

THERE IS A GREAT OPPORTUNITY TO USE PROVEN NUTRITION MODELS AND NEW FORMS OF CAPACITY BUILDING TO BOTH REDUCE INFANT SEVERE ACUTE MALNUTRITION AND EMPOWER WOMEN TO SUPPORT THEMSELVES AND THEIR FAMILIES...

DELIVERING SCALE, DIGNITY, AND SELF SUFFICIENCY.

We bring a network of proven producers of nutritional products for severely malnourished children, as well as the innovation in e- learning tools for training in various areas into a combined framework that delivers quality product to children in need, employment opportunities across the value chain, support to sustain an ARC-run virtual academy, and dignity and self-sufficiency.

Every aspect has been built in consideration with the people who use it—from the original conversations that led to the project, to the factory that will produce the Ready-to-Use Therapeutic Food (RUTF), to the online courses that will be offered at the e-learning academy. This project interweaves various cross-cutting themes to deliver an innovative combined network of social enterprise and virtual capacity building that is right in line with ARC’s aspiration of being a leading 21st century humanitarian organization.

The ChangeMaker Team

THROUGH CO-CREATION WITH THE WOMEN OF KALMA CAMP AND OTHER PARTNERS, WE WILL TACKLE MULTIPLE PROBLEMS WITH A SINGLE, MULTI-LAYERED, REALISTIC AND MEASURABLE APPROACH.

We also gathered a team of expert organizations to put together such a model.

Together we are able to combat some of the most daunting problems in Kalma camp, from malnutrition to lack of income-generation opportunities, while improving quality of living, helping women support themselves and their families, and assisting the restoration of their dignity and self-sufficiency.

The Goals

WE SET NEW PARADIGMS IN THE WAYS WE COMBAT CRITICAL PROBLEMS AND HELP CREATE NEW OPPORTUNITIES FOR THOSE WE SERVE IN KALMA CAMP

  • Create a self-sustaining model combining social enterprise and capacity building.
  • Reduce child malnutrition while creating opportunity for restoration of dignity and self-sufficiency through a co-creation approach.
  • Tackle multiple problems with a combined approach supported by meaningful partnerships with expert organizations in various fields.
  • Build capacity using technologically innovative tools to improve accessibility and reach of learning resources.
  • Inspire donors to get involved and reduce severe acute malnutrition while creating income-generation and women empowerment opportunities.
  • Lead the field through the construction of a scalable and replicable model for other organizations to use in their humanitarian settings.
